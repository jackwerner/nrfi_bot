Giants battery of Logan Webb and Patrick Bailey win Gold Gloves, first batterymates to do it together since Wainwright and Molina in 2013. Bailey's defense continues to impress while Webb adds to his growing reputation as one of the game's elite defenders on the mound.
Orioles decline $5.5M option on Jorge Mateo after injury-plagued season. The versatile speedster hit just .177 in 43 games while battling elbow and hamstring issues. He'll hit free agency for the first time at 31.
Mariners pick up option on dominant reliever Andres Munoz after stellar 2025 season. No surprise given his elite stuff, but bullpen arms are the foundation of Seattle's pitching strategy moving forward.
Brewers declined mutual options on Rhys Hoskins and Jose Quintana, making them free agents. They also turned down William Contreras' club option, but he stays with the team through arbitration. Milwaukee making early moves to reshape their roster for 2026.
Kyle Tucker headlines a stacked free agent class that just hit the market yesterday. Several teams already lining up for the star outfielder who's expected to command one of the biggest contracts of the offseason.
Jack Flaherty picking up his $20M player option with the Tigers for 2026. Solid move after posting a 4.64 ERA but strong peripherals with a 27.6% K rate. Detroit gets their rotation anchor back without hitting the crowded free agent market.
Yu Darvish out indefinitely with UCL injury, leaving Padres rotation in serious trouble heading into 2026. San Diego already losing Cease and King to free agency and now facing a major hole at the top of their staff.
Salvador Perez signs two-year, $25M extension with Royals through 2027. Nine-time All-Star catcher sticking with KC rather than having team just pick up his $13.5M option for next season.
