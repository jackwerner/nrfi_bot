Cardinals trading Sonny Gray to Red Sox for Fitts and Clarke plus $20M cash. Gray finally agreed to waive his no-trade after saying in September he'd "definitely consider" trades as St. Louis leans into rebuilding. Makes sense for both sides.
Paul Skenes received a record $3.4 million from the pre-arbitration bonus pool after his dominant rookie season with the Pirates. Guy's getting paid before he even hits arbitration.
Phil Maton just signed with the Cubs, bringing his elite curveball to Chicago's bullpen. Guy thrived last season with nearly 12 Ks per nine despite barely touching 90 with his cutter.
Red Sox showing interest in J.T. Realmuto despite expectations he'll return to Philadelphia. Boston could use a catching upgrade but might need to redirect funds if they can't pull Realmuto from the Phillies after his decade-plus of consistent production.
Jake Fraley back with the Rays after a bizarre three-week stretch where they claimed him off waivers, DFA'd him, then re-signed him. Versatile outfielder who can play all three positions despite injury history.
Angels and Rendon discussing retirement buyout with $38M still owed on his deal. Once a star with the Nats, injuries derailed his Angels tenure since 2020. His $245M contract will go down as one of the biggest busts in MLB history.
Orioles traded Grayson Rodriguez to the Angels for Taylor Ward in a surprising move. Rodriguez missed all of 2025 with elbow issues but has four years of team control, while Ward enters his age-32 season with just one year before free agency.
Dylan Cease just landed a seven-year, $210 million deal with the Blue Jays. Top arm on the market gets paid after making 32+ starts in each of the past five seasons.
