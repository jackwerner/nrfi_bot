Qualifying offer deadline hits today with several big names making decisions. Japanese star Tatsuya Imai drawing major interest from Yankees, Mets, Phillies and others after posting 1.92 ERA in Japan. Could be this winter's most sought-after pitcher.
Sandy Alcantara and Edward Cabrera are generating significant trade interest, with MLB Network insider Jon Paul Morosi reporting that both Marlins pitchers are "very popular" in the trade market. Morosi expressed confidence that Miami will trade one of these arms by mid-December.
Today's 6pm deadline for 40-man roster additions looms large as teams decide which prospects to protect from next month's Rule 5 Draft. Several notable names on the bubble across the league.
Trent Grisham, Gleyber Torres, Shota Imanaga and Brandon Woodruff all accepted their $22.025 million qualifying offers instead of testing free agency. Smart play for Woodruff coming off injury, but surprising to see Imanaga not pursue a multi-year deal after his All-Star season.
Orioles trade Grayson Rodriguez to Angels for Taylor Ward in the biggest move of deadline day. Rodriguez missed all of 2025 with multiple arm injuries but Baltimore's former top prospect is expected to be ready for spring training.
Japanese pitcher Tatsuya Imai officially posted for MLB teams yesterday. The 27-year-old Seibu Lions starter has drawn significant interest after being posted on November 10. Teams now have until January 2 to negotiate with the 2025 NPB standout.
Paul Skenes addresses trade rumors after Cy Young win, denying reports he wants out of Pittsburgh. Interesting timing with the Pirates needing to build around their ace rather than shopping him.
Blue Jays emerging as favorites to land Kyle Tucker according to rival executives. Toronto wants a left-handed bat to pair with Guerrero Jr after coming within inches of a World Series title in 2025.
