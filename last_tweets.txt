Twins reportedly keeping Buxton, Ryan, and López despite trade rumors. Front office wants to build around them for 2026 rather than continue the sell-off that saw 10 players moved at the deadline.
Zac Gallen drawing interest from Giants, Angels, Tigers, Orioles, Cubs and Braves after finishing 2025 strong. Rough start to the season (5.60 ERA through July) might actually keep his price reasonable despite the Cy Young pedigree.
Mets and Rangers swap Semien for Nimmo in a rare one-for-one trade of established veterans. Both players carrying hefty contracts, but the deal gives each team a different look heading into the Winter Meetings next week in Orlando.
Cody Bellinger drawing serious interest from Yankees, Mets, Dodgers and Phillies after hitting 29 homers with the Yankees last season. Big market teams all over the 30-year-old's versatility after his strong 2025 campaign.
Devin Williams agrees to three-year deal with Mets, who might not be done bolstering their bullpen. Even after landing the former Yankees closer who saved 18 games in 2025, New York still interested in bringing back Edwin Díaz.
Brewers reportedly "cracking the door open" on Freddy Peralta trade talks after significant interest from multiple teams. The 30-year-old righty still has two years and $43M left on his deal, making him one of the more intriguing arms potentially available.
Josh Naylor locks in with Mariners on a five-year, $92.5M deal with full no-trade clause. Guy stole 30 bases last season with 19 coming after his July trade to Seattle. Rare power-speed combo at first base.
Mariners just sent top catching prospect Harry Ford and pitcher Isaac Lyon to the Nationals for lefty reliever Jose Ferrer. Bold move giving up their best catching prospect for bullpen help after locking in Naylor at first base.
