Phil Maton signing with the Cubs on a two-year deal with a club option. Solid pickup for a Chicago bullpen that needs reliable arms after losing most of their playoff relief corps from last season.
Kazuma Okamoto and Kona Takahashi just got posted for MLB free agency, joining Munetaka Murakami and Tatsuya Imai from Japan. Teams looking for infield power or pitching depth have some interesting international options this winter.
Mets and Rangers just agreed to swap Marcus Semien for Brandon Nimmo. Big middle infield upgrade for New York while Texas gets the outfield help they needed. Rare star-for-star trade this early in the offseason.
Raisel Iglesias signs one-year, $16 million deal with the Braves. Atlanta's front office knew there might be long-term benefit when they didn't trade him at the deadline, and that patience paid off with both sides wanting to continue the relationship.
Joel Payamps signs with Braves on a one-year, $2.25 million deal. Solid pickup for Atlanta's bullpen after he posted strong strikeout numbers last season despite control issues.
Orioles traded Grayson Rodriguez to Angels for Taylor Ward in a surprising one-for-one swap. Baltimore gives up four years of team control for one season of Ward, while Angels get a high-upside arm coming back from injury to slot behind Kikuchi and Soriano.
Josh Naylor just locked in with the Mariners on a five-year, $92.5 million deal. First big signing of the offseason after he provided that spark down the stretch hitting .299 with 9 homers in 54 games for Seattle.
Michael King drawing serious interest from Marlins after posting a 2.93 ERA in 53 starts since moving to the rotation. His 2025 season was shortened by nerve and knee injuries, but that strikeout rate still makes him one of the more intriguing arms on the market.
